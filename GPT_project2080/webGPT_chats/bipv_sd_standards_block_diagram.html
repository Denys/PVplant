4. Cross-standard dependency logic

These are the interconnections between norms, not just between norms and hardware blocks.
Dependency

IEC 63092 sits on top of module safety / qualification

For a BIPV shading device, the building-product/system framing of IEC 63092 is not a substitute for the module safety and qualification basis delivered by IEC 61730 and IEC 61215.

Dependency

IEC 61853 feeds architecture modeling

Incidence-angle and temperature characterization are the model basis that should feed mismatch studies, façade yield estimates, and architecture scoring.

Dependency

IEC TS 63126 is a trigger, not a default claim

Open this path only when the BIPV shading device thermal envelope credibly exceeds the base qualification assumption.

Dependency

IEC 62548 consumes component-level interface choices

Connector, cable, feedthrough, and MLPE decisions close through IEC 62852, IEC 62930, IEC 62790, and IEC 62109-3 before the array design is actually defensible.

Dependency

IEC 62446 verifies the installed outcome

Commissioning, inspection, maintenance, and thermography close the loop on the choices made under IEC 63092, IEC 62548, and the product qualification standards.

Boundary note. This map is intentionally centered on the standards stack carried in the project pack. For a real project, the architecture also depends on standards/codes outside this requested set, especially low-voltage installation rules, standalone inverter product safety, grid interconnection, fire-service marking, and local façade/building regulations.
