Current Architecture Handout Set

Each branch preserves shading mitigation only when each blind module, or deliberate split section, gets independent MPPT before merge or conversion.

H1: Per-window DC/DC MPPT

A141/60
DC/DC window-level MPPT bus TBD

One DC/DC MPPT channel per blind/window, followed by a higher-voltage DC bus or multi-MPPT inverter at floor/facade level.

Target~30 Vmp legacy input; bus output architecture-defined
Power180-720 W envelope; 330-500 W practical focus
Blockercommercial blind-scale DC/DC product and service boundary

H2-PDO: Parallel-output DCO node

customopen
DC/DC parallel DCO no OTS closure

Per-blind MPPT DCO outputs merge into a protected 48-60 V common DC node. This is not raw PV paralleling and not a TS4/SolarEdge-style series optimizer.

Target36-40 Vmp, Voc cold <58 V
Power300-400 W/blind; split 600-720 W
Node48-60 V, usually 3-4 blinds at 400 W

H3: Microinverter AC branch

A236/60
DC/AC 1 MPPT input/blind grid profile

One blind per independent microinverter input. Preferred packaging is one fixed dual-input MI serving two blinds while keeping independent MPPT.

Target40 Vmp nominal, 38-42 V acceptable
Power300-480 W/blind, 400 W center
AC230 Vac 50 Hz for CH/EU certified product

H4/A4: Per-blind optimizer string

A445/60
optimizer facade inverter best evidence

One DC optimizer per whole blind, outputs in series into a DC string and facade/string inverter. This is the current H4/A4 branch from this chat.

Target45 Vmp nominal, 42-48 V acceptable
Power330-500 W/blind; 550-600 W edge
String12-14 blinds/string, 540-630 Vmp

Rules That Survive All Four Branches

  • One blind module means one whole PV blind/window assembly, not one slat.
  • Do not merge differently shaded blinds before independent MPPT.
  • Keep power electronics in fixed serviceable frame/headbox/facade zones.
  • The moving interface should carry protected PV DC only unless a full AC moving-interface safety case is built.
  • Vendor data must close Voc/cold, Isc/max, Vmp/Imp/Pmp, thermal envelope, connector, cable, feedthrough, shutdown, and replacement boundary.

Fast Read

  • H4/A4 has the strongest off-the-shelf device and price evidence.
  • H3 is real and mature but pulls AC branch, grid-code, gateway, and service complexity into the facade.
  • H2-PDO is a serious custom electronics branch, not a near-term component-selection branch.
  • H1 remains the generic per-window DC/DC baseline but needs a selected converter and bus architecture.
