Most suitable now

Series DCO / optimizer string because it combines blind-level MPPT, fixed service placement, known MLPE products, and the strongest power-device price evidence.

Most interesting custom path

Parallel DCO / PDO because it matches the Notebook decks' native-BIPV logic, but it needs a certified per-blind DCO with reverse blocking, current limiting, bus control, thermal closure, and RSD strategy.

Rejected pattern

Any topology that merges differently shaded blinds before independent MPPT. That includes raw parallel PV buses and coarse zone collectors without verified shade homogeneity.

Notebook And Literature-Style Synthesis

The three NotebookLM/PVBrain PDFs explain the same architecture space from a more conceptual BIPV perspective. They are useful as synthesis sources, not supplier evidence.

Cross-source consensus

  • Granular MPPT is the decisive feature for dynamic BIPV shading.
  • Module/blind-level tracking beats zone-level tracking when shading is non-uniform.
  • DC optimizer variants tend to be lower in power-device cost than MI variants.
  • Parallel MI is strongest for DC safety and independence, but highest in device count and AC facade burden.
  • Zone architectures reduce devices but trade away diagnostics, fault isolation, and shade granularity.

Preserved disagreement

  • Firecrawl/vendor evidence: series optimizer string leads today.
  • NOTEBOOK and NOTEBOOK2: parallel DCO/PDO is the conceptual optimum.
  • PVBRAIN: parallel MI is the safety/yield maximum; zone MI is a pragmatic lower-device branch.
  • Dashboard v2 conclusion: choose series DCO now; develop or request evidence for PDO next.
